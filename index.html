Analytical framework

Frame: what type of manipulation does each actor deploy and for what strategic purpose? Not "who lies more." All parties are engaged in active information operations. No single source should be treated as authoritative without cross-referencing.

Fabrication
Producing or amplifying content that is demonstrably false — invented events, fake quotes, or wholly fictitious claims presented as fact.
Manipulation
Taking real events or statements and distorting them — through selective editing, misleading framing, false context, or exaggeration — to create a false impression without outright fabricating facts.
Narrative management
Deliberately shaping how a story is understood — through consistent messaging, strategic emphasis, or coordinated framing — without necessarily fabricating anything. The goal is to control interpretation rather than invent facts.
Structural
Bias or distortion that is built into the media environment itself — not a deliberate act by an individual but a systemic pattern, such as which stories get covered, whose voices are platformed, or what is treated as the default assumption. Examples: a state broadcaster that never questions the government; a media ecosystem that structurally excludes opposition voices.

About this dashboard

This dashboard is produced through structured scanning and synthesis of publicly available, open-source reporting across regional and international media ecosystems. The objective is to provide a consolidated, cross-perspective view of fast-moving developments, prioritising Arabic-language and Middle East regional media sources.

Sourcing & analytical method
Source Coverage

The analysis draws on English-language reporting as well as Arabic-language and Farsi-language sources. This multilingual approach captures differences in framing, emphasis, and narrative across state, regional, and international media environments. "Farsi" is used throughout in preference to "Persian-language" when describing Iranian-language media.

Source Selection

The dashboard prioritises outlets with consistent coverage of Middle East affairs. Sources are not treated as equally reliable — they are used comparatively to identify convergence, divergence, and narrative intent. All outlets are annotated with funding source and editorial orientation before citation.

Analytical Method

Content is synthesised using structured prompting techniques designed to extract the most policy-relevant developments across military activity, diplomatic signalling, energy impacts, and information operations. Contradictions across sources are preserved and flagged rather than resolved artificially. The disinformation frame asks: what type of manipulation does each actor deploy and for what strategic purpose? — not "who lies more."

Cross-Source Validation

Where possible, claims are triangulated across multiple outlets. When verification is not possible, items are explicitly labelled as unverified, contested, or narrative-driven. All casualty figures are noted as contested, with multiple source versions — IRNA official / HRANA / Red Crescent / health ministries.

Standing Editorial Rules
  • UN diplomatic tracks are never omitted — Arnault, UNOPS Task Force, and UNSC resolutions appear in every update
  • Three diplomatic tracks always distinguished: (a) UN/multilateral institutional, (b) multilateral state initiatives, (c) bilateral channels
  • Gaza linkage always included when covering Arab diplomatic positioning
  • Funding and editorial orientation flagged before citing any outlet
  • Casualty figures always noted as contested with multiple source versions
  • "Farsi" used in preference to "Persian-language"
Use of generative AI
Role of AI

Generative AI (Claude Sonnet 4.6, Anthropic) is used as an analytical aid to organise, synthesise, and structure large volumes of open-source information. It does not replace source material or independent verification.

Inputs & Limitations

All underlying inputs are derived from publicly available reporting. No classified or proprietary data is used. AI-generated outputs are dependent on the quality and diversity of source inputs. Outputs may contain omissions or misinterpretations and should be treated as analytical support, not definitive intelligence. Human oversight is required to interpret outputs appropriately.

Prompt Engineering

Prompts are engineered to produce a comprehensive multi-source political intelligence brief prioritising Arabic-language and Middle East regional media sources. The system prompt enforces consistent editorial rules across every update cycle.

Responsible use
Intended Use

This dashboard is intended for informational and analytical purposes only. Users should apply independent judgement and, where necessary, consult primary sources before drawing conclusions or making decisions.

Contested Information Environment

This conflict is occurring in an extremely high-noise, high-disinformation environment. All parties — including state media, opposition media, and Western sources — are engaged in active information operations. No single source should be treated as authoritative without cross-referencing.

No Classified Data

All content is based entirely on open-source reporting. No intelligence community sources, government-restricted data, or proprietary databases are used.
